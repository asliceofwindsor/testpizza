Italian Immigration to Canada

Italian immigrants arrived in Canada and the United States in two waves during the Twentieth Century, primarily driven by economic opportunities. The first wave was between the early 1900s and World War I. The second wave was primarly after World War II.

In the first wave of Italian migration to Canada Italian migrants immigrated in search of labour. Immigrants were drawn by opportunities to construct transcontinental railroads, grain elevators, work in mines, and work as tradesmen. Immigrants worked in harsh and dangerous condtions while also facing discrimination.

Many of the 60,000 Italian Immigrants settled in Ontario. As postions were often seasonal and harsh immigrants often moved back to Italy or established roots in Canada. Many families looking to feed their families moved to cities like Windsor. Windsor was ideal due to its proximity to Detroit, and as the city's automotive and construction industries grew, so did the Italian population.

Although many worked in factories or construction, some began their businesses. As many Italians moved back to Italy, some stayed in cities like Windsor in search of economic opportunities. A culture of hard work, dedication, and commitment to family flourished and quickly became pillars of business success.

Early Italian Run Buisnesses

Mike Mario came to Canada in 1910, at the age of 15, to help build a railway. Mario later moved to Montreal and developed his love for cooking.

Mike then moved to Windsor in 1923 and later opened his restaurant in 1928, which served a variety of foods, including pizza. In 1930, the restaurant moved locations from the British American Hotel to 493 Ouellette Ave. At the height of its popularity, there were 3 restaurants operating within the city. Although their roast beef was popular, the business was also known for its pizza. At one point, the owners shipped 40 of their pizzas to a couple in Texas.

The diner was known to attract entertainers from the Elmwood Motel, such as Cher. Mario's story identifies the early growth of an Italian-run business. These businesses worked to create family environments through their diner-style format and customer relationships. Mike, who came from gruelling railway work, developed a love for cooking and put in countless hours running his business. Mario's restaurant highlights the early success of an Italian-run business in Windsor that served pizza and gained notoriety.

Immigration in America Compared to Windsor

In the United States, many Italians also migrated for new economic opportunities; unlike Windsor, Italians in Detroit did not have long-lasting areas of Italian culture. As the twentieth century approached, many Italians migrated to Michigan due to the economic hardship following Italy's unification. Due to labour demand in Michigan cities and mining towns, this area was the perfect destination.

Compared to Windsor, Detroit attracted many Italian immigrants due to the booming manufacturing and service industry. Italians in Detroit also created hubs for Italians by opening shops, restaurants, and pizzerias along Gratiot Avenue, creating a "Little Italy."

Due to America's melting-pot nature, many Italians in Detroit assimilated into American culture and moved away from their neighbourhoods once gaining more economic freedom. In contrast, Italians in Windsor created a unique centre of Italian culture on Erie Street. Although Detroit experienced significant migration during the early 1900s, far greater than in Windsor, the longstanding American identity and Detroit's multicultural space encouraged cultural assimilation among Italians.

Foundations for Windsor Pizza

It was not until the second wave of Italian Immigration where much of Windsors Italian idenity was formed. However, these early immigrant communtiees laid the foundations for Italian communities to develop their identity in a distinct space. Therefore, early migration to Windsor and the availability of job opportunities laid the foundation for Italian business. This foundation encouraged the expression and integration of Italian culture in Windsor pizzerias, making it part of Windsor's identity.
